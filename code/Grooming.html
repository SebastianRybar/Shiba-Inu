Grooming

The Essential Guide to Grooming Your Shiba Inu


Brushing

Frequency: Brush your Shiba Inu at least twice a week. During shedding seasons, which occur twice a year (spring and fall), daily brushing may be necessary.

Tools: Use a slicker brush or a deshedding tool to remove loose fur from the undercoat. A bristle brush can help smooth the outer coat and distribute natural oils.

Bathing

Frequency: Shiba Inus are generally clean dogs and don’t require frequent baths. Bathing once every 3-4 months is usually sufficient unless they get particularly dirty.

Products: Use a gentle, dog-specific shampoo to avoid drying out their skin. Make sure to rinse thoroughly to prevent any residue from causing irritation.

Shedding

Management: During shedding seasons, be prepared for a significant amount of fur loss. Increase brushing frequency and consider using a blower (like a pet dryer) to help remove loose fur.
