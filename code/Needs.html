Understanding the Unique Needs of Shiba Inus


Training and Commands

Basic Obedience: Start with basic obedience training, teaching commands like sit, stay, and come. This provides mental stimulation and helps manage their behavior.

Advanced Training: Engage them in advanced training or agility courses to challenge their intelligence and keep them mentally active.

Games and Challenges

Hide and Seek: Play hide and seek with treats or toys to engage their sense of smell and problem-solving abilities.

Scent Work: Introduce scent work activities where they have to find hidden objects using their nose.

Early Socialization

Exposure: Expose your Shiba Inu to various environments, people, and other animals from a young age to help them become well-adjusted adults.

Positive Experiences: Ensure that their early socialization experiences are positive to build their confidence and reduce anxiety.

Regular Interaction

Dog Parks: Regular visits to dog parks can help your Shiba Inu interact with other dogs, improving their social skills.

Playdates: Arrange playdates with other dogs to provide controlled social interaction and help them learn appropriate behavior.
